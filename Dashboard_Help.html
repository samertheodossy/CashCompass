Getting started

  1. Open Setup / Review from the top-right.
  2. Add your bank accounts, debts, bills, income, and profile information.
  3. Choose Refresh Financial Plan after your starting information is ready.
  4. Open Planning → Next Actions for a short, prioritized list.

Upcoming expenses and properties are optional. Add them when they are useful to your household plan.

Setup / Review

Setup / Review shows what is ready and what still needs attention. It does not change your data by itself.

  • Not set up means CashCompass still needs information for that area.
  • Setup complete means enough information is available to use that area.
  • Open a card to review its summary and continue to the matching editor.
  • Your profile supplies the name and email used for Financial Plan messages. Date of birth supports retirement calculations.
  • When all required areas are complete, use Go to Next Actions to continue.

Safety: Setup / Review never changes your data on its own. An editor saves only after you choose its Save or Add action.

Overview

Overview summarizes cash, investments, real estate, debt, net worth, financial health, retirement, near-term obligations, and suggested actions.

  • Change vs prior period compares current information with the most relevant earlier Financial Plan refresh.
  • Financial Health explains the main factors helping or hurting the score.
  • Operations Snapshot highlights bills, upcoming expenses, and property spending.
  • Weekly net worth change appears after Financial Plan history is available.
  • Suggested Actions offers practical next steps. Issues keeps the complete warning list separate.

Assets & Liabilities

Bank accounts

  • Update records a balance for the selected month.
  • Available Now is the amount that may be considered for bills or debt decisions.
  • Min Buffer protects the minimum balance you want to keep.
  • Use policy tells the planner whether an account is protected or available for a specific purpose.
  • Stop tracking removes an account from active views while preserving past balances.

Investments

  • Record each investment account and its current value.
  • Use a clear type such as Brokerage, Retirement, or Education.
  • Stopping tracking preserves past values and removes the account from active totals.

House values

  • Record the property value and remaining loan amount.
  • CashCompass calculates equity from those two amounts.
  • Each property can also track repairs, fees, insurance, and other expenses.

Debt accounts

  • Update balances, minimum payments, interest rates, limits, and due dates.
  • These current values feed Debt overview, Rolling debt payoff, and Net Worth.
  • Stopping tracking preserves history while removing the debt from active totals.

Cash Flow

Cash Flow brings together payments, upcoming expenses, donations, bills, and income.

  • Quick add records an income or expense amount for a selected month.
  • Income tracks recurring and other household income.
  • Bills manages recurring obligations and their schedules.
  • Upcoming keeps larger planned expenses visible before they arrive.
  • Donations records charitable giving by tax year.

Upcoming expenses

  • Add the expense name, due date, amount, payment source, and optional notes.
  • Quick add payment records a payment without retyping the details.
  • Dismiss removes an item from active planning while preserving its history.
  • The 7-day, 30-day, overdue, and total-planned cards update from active items.

Donations

Use Donations as a charitable-giving record for reporting and tax preparation. Choose a tax year, charity, date, amount, and optional comments.

A donation is not automatically treated as a Cash Flow payment. Record the payment separately when you want it included in monthly Cash Flow.

Activity

Activity shows important actions recorded through CashCompass, including payments, bill changes, property expenses, donations, and account updates.

  • Use filters and sorting to find an action.
  • Activity is an audit trail; it is not a financial statement.
  • Removing an eligible mistaken activity entry does not silently rewrite unrelated financial records.

Bills

  • Due Soon and Overdue show obligations that still need attention.
  • Pay records the payment and refreshes the affected views.
  • Skip suppresses only the selected occurrence.
  • Autopay identifies bills expected to be handled automatically.
  • Weekly and biweekly bills use their configured weekday and schedule anchor when available.
  • Manage bills edits schedules, amounts, sources, and tracking status.

Properties

  • House Expenses records repairs, maintenance, fees, and insurance details.
  • Property performance separates operating income and expenses from actual linked loan payments, then shows after-financing net cash flow.
  • Use consistent property names so income and expenses are grouped correctly.

Property performance

Review each property’s current value, loan balance, equity, rental income, operating expenses, actual linked loan payments, and after-financing net cash flow.

Loan payments use recorded Cash Flow amounts for active Loan or HELOC accounts linked under Planning → Debts. Missing or ambiguous payments stay at $0.00 with guidance rather than being estimated.

Planning

Planning turns your current financial information into decisions. Start with Next Actions, then open deeper views when you need them.

  • Next Actions prioritizes immediate work.
  • Debts maintains balances, rates, minimums, due dates, and tracking status. For a Loan or HELOC, Linked Property can optionally connect it to an active property; other debt types remain unlinked.
  • Debt Overview compares payoff paths.
  • Rolling Debt Payoff builds a month-by-month operating plan.
  • Retirement evaluates your selected assumptions and scenarios.
  • Purchase Simulator explores affordability without changing saved account balances.

Next actions

Next Actions is the best place to begin after setup. It combines near-term obligations, cash available after protections, and planning priorities.

  • Address overdue or urgent items first.
  • Review the reason shown before acting on a suggested payment.
  • Use the linked workspace button to inspect or update the underlying information.

Debt overview

Debt Overview compares balances, required payments, payoff timing, and interest impact. It is a planning view, not a promise of future results.

Update balances and interest rates, then refresh the Financial Plan before making a major payoff decision.

Rolling debt payoff

This view estimates how much cash can be used after protected balances, buffers, upcoming expenses, and other near-term risks.

  • Safe to use is the maximum amount available after protections.
  • Cash to use now is your chosen amount, capped at the safe limit.
  • Standard payoff follows the normal priority model.
  • Aggressive payoff explores faster allocation while keeping the same safety cap.
  • Show details explains assumptions and forecasts for users who want a deeper review.

Financial Plan email

  • Email recipients come from the primary and spouse/partner email fields in your Profile.
  • If both email fields are blank, no message is sent.
  • Background saves group changes together to avoid repeated messages.
  • Refresh Financial Plan requests an immediate current result.
  • The email summarizes urgent bills, upcoming obligations, cash flow, and payoff guidance. Use CashCompass for the full explanation.
